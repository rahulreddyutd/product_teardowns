LinkedIn

Creator Mode launched in 2021. Millions opted in. Most stopped posting within weeks. Here is why — and what to fix.

Retention Creator Mode Improvement teardown
Phase 1 — Overview
What is LinkedIn
The world's largest professional network. 1B+ members. Revenue from talent solutions, marketing, and Premium subscriptions. All three depend on one thing: active, high-quality content keeping people coming back.
What is Creator Mode
Launched 2021. Converts a standard profile into a content-first presence. Follow button replaces connect. Better algorithmic distribution. Access to LinkedIn Live and newsletters.

The problem: Creator Mode has a drop-off problem hiding inside an activation success. Creators opt in, post actively for 2–3 weeks, then go quiet. The feed fills back up with work anniversaries. LinkedIn invested in creator tools that most creators stopped using after the first month.

Who
Professionals who opted into Creator Mode — consultants, founders, PMs, engineers — who want to build a professional audience.
Why it matters
Better content = more engagement = more ad impressions + more Premium conversions. Creator retention is a direct revenue lever LinkedIn is currently underinvesting in.

Maya represents the largest group of Creator Mode users — genuine intent, early activation, rapid drop-off. Solving for Maya partially solves for James. Priya already has a system and needs different tools.

Phase 5 — Solutions + hypotheses
Priority 1 · Ship first
Post performance benchmark card
Immediately after publishing show the post's performance vs the creator's own average across their last 10 posts. "Your last 5 posts averaged 180 impressions in the first hour. This post has 340."
HypothesisIf creators see their post benchmarked against their own average immediately after publishing, posting frequency will increase 20% in 30 days because creators will understand what good looks like for them specifically.
Priority 1 · Ship first
Weekly creator digest
Every Monday: top performing post from last week, engagement rate vs average, best day and time for their specific audience, one actionable suggestion for the week ahead.
HypothesisIf creators receive a weekly personalized digest, posting frequency will increase by at least once more per week because the digest provides insight and social proof of what peers are doing.
Priority 2 · Phase 2
Topic tagging + performance history
Allow creators to tag posts by topic. After 10 posts surface a breakdown: "Posts about career advice average 2x more engagement than posts about product strategy for your audience."
HypothesisIf creators can see which topics resonate with their specific audience, content quality and posting frequency will both increase because creators have a clear signal about what to write next.
Priority 3 · Phase 3
Creator home dashboard
A creator-specific home view that surfaces insights, suggested topics, and posting streaks in one place. Less push notification, more pull — creators who open the app see their data front and center.
HypothesisIf creators have a dedicated home view that surfaces actionable insights every time they open LinkedIn, daily active usage among Creator Mode users will increase 30%.
Phase 6 — MVP + go-to-market
1
Post-publish benchmark card
Appears immediately after publishing. Shows performance in the first hour compared to creator's own average across last 10 posts. No external benchmarks — just the creator's own data in context. Edge case: fewer than 5 posts → show a message explaining how many more are needed before benchmarks activate.
2
Weekly creator digest
Sent every Monday to Creator Mode users who posted at least once in the last 30 days. Top performing post, engagement rate vs average, and one actionable suggestion. Not sent to users who have not posted in 30 days — they have already churned and a digest email will feel irrelevant.
Go-to-market strategy
Launch audience
Creator Mode users who have posted 5–20 times — active enough for benchmark data, not so active they have their own system
Messaging
"Your creator insights are ready" — not new feature language. Frame as surfacing data that was always there.
Launch channel
In-app notification to pilot group before any external announcement
Success metric
15%+ increase in posting frequency in 30 days post-launch vs 30 days pre-launch → roll out to all Creator Mode users
Notion

35M+ users. The most powerful note-taking app ever built. And somehow, most new users quit before they build anything useful.

Activation Onboarding Improvement teardown
Phase 1 — Overview
What is Notion
All-in-one workspace combining notes, docs, databases, and project management. 35M+ users. Free tier drives top-of-funnel. Converts to paid when teams need collaboration or hit storage limits.
The activation problem
Users who do not create something meaningful in their first 7 days are significantly less likely to return. The product can do everything — which means new users do not know where to start.

The hypothesis: Based on publicly available user feedback and product analysis, new users appear to struggle with the blank canvas — they sign up expecting a better Google Docs and leave before ever building anything useful. This teardown explores what the activation failure looks like and how to fix it.

Phase 5 — Solutions + hypotheses
Priority 1 · Ship first

After selecting use case at signup, show exactly 3 templates with a one-line explanation of why each fits. Remove the full gallery from the onboarding flow. Let users explore it later when they know what they are doing.

Priority 1 · Ship first
Guided first page with prompts
Replace the blank canvas with a structured first page for new users. 4 prompts that fade as the user fills them in. Users who want blank canvas can clear prompts in one click.
HypothesisIf new users land on a guided first page, pages created in the first session will increase 50% because the prompts reduce the activation energy required to start.
Priority 1 · Ship first
Onboarding checklist in sidebar
Persistent but dismissable checklist. 5 items. Each links to a specific action. Disappears after 30 days or all items are checked.
HypothesisIf new users have an onboarding checklist, return visit rate in the first 14 days will increase 30% because the checklist gives users a clear reason to come back.
Priority 2 · Phase 2
Setup wizard
A 3-step wizard walking new users through creating their first page, their first database, and their first linked view. Short, opinionated, with a clear end state.
HypothesisIf new users go through a 3-step setup wizard, activation rate measured by pages created in the first 7 days will increase 35%.
Phase 6 — MVP + go-to-market
1
3-template recommendation screen
Replace the template gallery in onboarding with 3 curated recommendations based on stated use case. Add "Browse all templates" link at the bottom. The full gallery stays — just moved out of the critical onboarding path.
2
Guided first page
4 structured prompts for new users that fade as they are filled in. Users who want blank canvas clear them in one click. Returning users and users creating new workspaces bypass this entirely.
3
Sidebar onboarding checklist
5 items linking to specific actions. Disappears after 30 days or completion. Users who dismiss it do not see it again.
Go-to-market strategy
Test method
A/B test new onboarding vs current for all new signups. 50/50 split for 4 weeks.
Primary metric
Pages created in first 7 days
Secondary metric
30-day retention rate
Ship threshold
20%+ increase in pages created in first 7 days → roll out to 100% of new users
Waze

The entire competitive moat is community reporting. And the people who report most are quietly losing motivation.

Retention Community reporting Improvement teardown
Phase 1 — Overview
What is Waze
Community-based navigation app. 140M+ monthly active users. Revenue from map advertising and data licensing. Owned by Google since 2013. The only differentiator vs Google Maps is the community reporting system.
Why this matters
Remove the reporters and Waze becomes an inferior version of Google Maps with a worse base map. The community is not a feature — it is the entire product thesis. Losing committed reporters is an existential risk.

The hypothesis: Active reporting appears to be declining on a per-user basis based on publicly discussed patterns. Waze has grown its user base but the ratio of reporters to passengers may be shifting toward passengers. This teardown explores what is driving reporter disengagement and what to build to fix it.

Make voice reporting the primary method with a simple, consistent phrase — "Hey Waze, police on my right" — that works reliably every time. Rebuilt specifically for the 8 most common report types.

2

Sent every Monday to users who submitted at least 3 reports in the previous week. Below 3 reports the numbers are too small to feel meaningful — threshold is intentional.

Figma

One of the most generous free tiers in software. Which is exactly why active daily users never upgrade.

Revenue Freemium conversion Improvement teardown
Phase 1 — Overview
What is Figma
Browser-based collaborative design tool. Dominant in product design. Free tier: unlimited editors, unlimited collaborators, 3 project limit. Professional tier: $12/editor/month. Organization: $45/editor/month.
The conversion problem
The 3-project limit is a friction point but not a pain point. Teams work around it by reorganizing existing projects rather than paying to remove the constraint. The free tier is good enough to never upgrade.

The hypothesis: Free tier users are performing Professional-level work with manual workarounds every week — branching files manually, managing version history by hand — and Figma has no mechanism to show them this is happening or offer a better path. The upgrade prompt fires at the wrong moment (frustration) rather than desire.

Duolingo

The streak is one of the most effective retention tools ever built. It is also creating users who are engaged but not learning.

Retention Streak mechanic Improvement teardown
Phase 1 — Overview
What is Duolingo
Most downloaded education app. 500M+ registered users. 40M+ DAU. Revenue from advertising (free tier) and Super Duolingo subscriptions ($6.99/month). Business model is deeply tied to DAU — more daily users = more ad impressions + more premium conversions.
The streak tension
The streak rewards consistency of engagement, not quality of engagement. The minimum to maintain a streak is one lesson. Many users complete exactly one lesson and close the app. 200-day streak. Minimal Spanish improvement.

The hypothesis: Users with long streaks appear to be optimizing for streak maintenance rather than language learning — choosing the easiest available lesson every day, completing the minimum, and closing the app. When the streak breaks they churn because the streak was the product, not the language. This teardown explores how to fix that without destroying the retention behavior that makes the streak valuable to the business.

Phase 2 — User personas

Phase 5 — Solutions + hypotheses
Priority 1 · Ship first
Adaptive difficulty recommendation
Change the recommendation algorithm to weight toward challenging content for users with 30+ day streaks. Surface easy review lessons as an option but not as the primary recommendation. Users who have demonstrated consistency should be pushed harder.
HypothesisIf the algorithm weights toward challenging content for streak users, course progression rate will increase 25% and language ability scores on benchmark assessments will improve 15%.
Priority 1 · Ship first
Monthly language ability check-in
On the first day of each month prompt users with 30+ day streaks to complete a 10-question benchmark assessment. Show how their score changed over the last 3 months. Gives Michael concrete evidence of whether his streak is producing real learning.
HypothesisIf users complete a monthly benchmark and see language ability progress over time, 90-day retention will increase 15% because users who can see real progress are more motivated than those who only see streak numbers.
Priority 1 · Ship first
Learning history preservation after streak break
When a streak breaks show a new screen: "Your streak is starting over. But your 180 days of learning are still with you." Below it: everything learned — vocabulary, grammar concepts, units completed. Shift the frame from loss to continuity.
HypothesisIf users see their learning history preserved after a streak break, churn rate after streak loss events will decrease 30% because the emotional devastation is mitigated by the preservation of real learning history.
Priority 2 · Phase 2
Conversation readiness score
A percentage on the home screen estimating how prepared the user is for a basic conversation — based on vocabulary coverage, grammar accuracy, and lesson progression. Updated weekly. The score that actually matters.
HypothesisIf users see a Conversation Readiness score measuring actual language ability, session depth will increase 20% because users will engage more seriously when they can see the direct impact on a meaningful outcome.

First day of each month. 10-question benchmark. Results stored and shown as a progress chart. 5 minutes. Skippable but shown prominently. Only for users with 30+ day streaks who have enough history for meaningful comparison.
3
Learning history preservation
When streak breaks: new screen showing everything the user has learned. Frame shifts from "you lost 180 days" to "your 180 days of learning are still with you." The streak resets. The learning does not.
Go-to-market strategy
Launch audience
Users with 60–180 day streaks — long enough for meaningful data, not so long they are the most vocal community members who might resist change
Messaging
"New learning insights" — not "we changed the streak." The streak still exists. Now there is more to the story.
Primary metric
Course progression rate (units completed per week)
Secondary metric
90-day retention after streak break events
